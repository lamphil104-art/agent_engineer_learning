AI agents are systems that use language models, tools, and workflows to solve tasks. 
Unlike simple chatbots, they can choose actions, use external tools, and produce structured outputs.

Retrieval-Augmented Generation, or RAG, helps AI systems answer questions using external knowledge. 
Instead of depending only on the model's internal knowledge, the system first retrieves relevant text from documents.

Chunking is a key step in RAG. Long documents should be split into smaller chunks so retrieval can find the most relevant pieces. 
If chunks are too small, they lose context. If they are too large, they may contain too much irrelevant information.

Good chunking improves retrieval quality and helps the model generate better answers.
